AI Opportunity Cards

UiLab Open House · July 2026 · 11 sector examples

How to use these cards. Browse them individually. Find the card closest to your work — or use them to check and sharpen a task you already have in mind. If your task is not represented here, write it in your own words at the top of your Agent Brief. One task only. Depth over breadth.
01
Healthcare & clinical services
Nothing Gets Missed at Handover
Clinical staff · Allied health · Nursing
Current process
Review patient notes and shift observations before handover. Compile actions, medication changes, and alerts for incoming staff. Deliver verbal or written handover under time pressure at shift end.
Why it is painful
Quality varies with how rushed the shift has been. Critical information is sometimes omitted. Completing documentation after a demanding shift takes time that staff rarely have.
Knowledge sources
Shift observations and notes, medication updates, flagged concerns, ward context
What the agent does
Structures a clear, consistent handover summary flagging priority patients, pending actions, and clinical alerts — ready to review before handover
Human still decides
Clinical judgment, escalation decisions, verbal handover emphasis, final accuracy check
Example agent name
Nothing Gets Missed at Handover
Complete, consistent handover summary ready in under 5 minutes at shift end

Disability support & NDIS services
Every Session Gets Recorded
Support workers · Coordinators · Team leaders
Current process
Complete a support session with a participant. Write a progress note aligned to NDIS outcomes and participant goals. Flag concerns, update goal progress, and submit within the required timeframe.
Why it is painful
Writing NDIS-aligned notes consistently after every session is time-consuming. Language and format requirements are specific. Quality and compliance vary across a large workforce.
Knowledge sources
Participant goals, session observations, NDIS outcome domain, support plan
What the agent does
Drafts an NDIS-aligned progress note in appropriate professional language, linking session observations to participant goals and outcomes
Human still decides
Accuracy of observations, safeguarding judgments, what to include or exclude, final review before submission
Example agent name
Every Session Gets Recorded
Compliant progress note drafted in 5 minutes after each session
03
Engineering & project management
Never Miss a Milestone
Project managers · Engineers · Consultants
Current process
Compile updates from team members. Review meeting notes, task trackers, and site reports. Write the client or internal status update. Repeat weekly or fortnightly across multiple concurrent projects.
Why it is painful
Each update takes 60–90 minutes per project. Tone and format vary by author. Items are missed under deadline pressure. Consistency across a portfolio is hard to maintain.
Knowledge sources
Meeting notes, milestone tracker, risk register, previous update, project and stakeholder names
What the agent does
Synthesises inputs, flags risks and blockers, drafts a structured status update in a consistent professional format
Human still decides
Risk escalation decisions, client relationship tone, technical accuracy, final sign-off
Example agent name
Never Miss a Milestone
Weekly project status update drafted in 10 minutes instead of 60–90
